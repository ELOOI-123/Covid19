COVID 19 Data Explorer

This page demonstrates the updated data on COVID 19 around the world .

COVID-19 Cases Worldwide

In this data visualization, we present the global impact of the COVID-19 pandemic as of April 10, 2023, using data sourced from 'Our World in Data.' The first visualization is a choropleth map that provides a visual representation of the total number of COVID-19 cases that have occurred worldwide. Each country or region is color-coded to reflect the severity of the pandemic, with darker shades indicating higher case counts. This map offers a comprehensive view of the spread and distribution of COVID-19 cases across the globe.

Total COVID-19 Cases and Deaths

This visualization takes a closer look at the relationship between total COVID-19 cases and total deaths. Using a bubble plot, we plot each country or region on a graph, with the total cases on the x-axis and total deaths on the y-axis. Each data point represents a specific location, and the size of the bubble corresponds to the magnitude of the outbreak. By visually aligning total cases and deaths, this visualization allows us to examine the impact of the pandemic in different regions, highlighting areas with higher case fatality rates.

Growth of COVID-19 Cases Over the Years

For a broader perspective, this line chart presents a time-series analysis of COVID-19 cases worldwide. This chart shows the evolution of the pandemic by displaying the total number of cases reported annually, spanning multiple years. By examining trends and patterns over time, we gain insights into the progression of the pandemic on a global scale. This visualization aids in understanding the ebb and flow of the pandemic, as well as any long-term impacts on public health and healthcare systems
